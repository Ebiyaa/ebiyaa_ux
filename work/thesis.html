Exploring the Future of Digital Identity & Autonomous
AI Agents in Decentralised Financial Systems

The Provocation

After spending a chunk of my career building financial products, I started to wonder what the next decade of finance might look like. The rise of decentralised finance, the emergence of autonomous AI agents, and the increasing integration of blockchain technology have created a new paradigm for financial systems. This paradigm challenges traditional notions of trust, accountability, and human oversight, raising critical questions about the ethical and societal implications of these technologies.

This research starts from a single scenario: receiving a rejection letter not from a bank, but from a protocol. No human reviewed it. No appeal exists. It isn't science fiction but it's the logical extension of systems already being built. The thesis, Exploring the Future of Digital Identity and Autonomous AI Agents in Decentralised Financial Systems Through Speculative Design, asks what it would actually feel like to live inside a world where identity, decentralised finance, and autonomous AI agents converge.

Research Questions

1. How do people respond emotionally to identity-gated financial systems, and what trust and accountability would they require?

2. Where do people draw the line on delegating financial agency to autonomous AI?

3. Can speculative artifacts work as research instruments for surfacing these ethics, and what design principles follow?

The study doesn't build financial technology. It builds the experiential surface of a possible future, and asks real people to inhabit it.

Why Design, Not Just Analysis

The theoretical grounding draws on Weyl, Ohlhaver and Buterin's Soulbound Tokens; permanent, on-chain credentials whose very durability is what makes a revoked one so damaging, and Ovadya and Siddarth's ETHOS framework for decentralised AI governance, alongside a deliberate parallel to China's Social Credit System and the shared logic of quantified trustworthiness.

This research uses speculative and critical design (Dunne & Raby, 2013) as research-through-design: building artifacts that make a possible future tangible, then placing participants inside it to see what they trust, fear, and would refuse.

The Speculative Artifact System

Participants encounter a set of interconnected artifacts, each surfacing a different pressure point: a Soul Document (an on-chain identity passport with soulbound token badges), an Algorithmic Exclusion Notice (a rejection letter with no appeal, handed to participants before they understand why), a Financial Delegation Contract giving an AI agent the power to defer their own override requests, and an Agent Activity Statement annotated with the agent's reasoning.

Interactive Installation

An sensor-triggered scan installation plays a three-stage video sequence (via Arduino and TouchDesigner) tracing the evolving relationship between a user and their AI agent. The system features alongside a physical agent object that changes state; calm, then erratic, then urgent, across three scenarios: identity registration and delegation, the agent operating with a widening and invisible gap between action and understanding, and finally the agent evolving beyond its parameters and locking the user out. A governance failure, not a technology failure.

This project asks a question I carry into every product I design: not whether a system is technically possible, but what it actually produces for the people who have to live inside it.
